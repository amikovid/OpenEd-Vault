### Vid-SecretWeapon-V1 - Worst CPA ($220.48)
**Format:** Vertical video with moody background footage of woman sipping coffee, text overlay
**Copy on creative:**
> "She had laminated schedules. I had coffee stains and confidence. She worried about Pinterest perfect photos. I thought of the moments no camera could capture. She asked my favorite curriculum. I slid my phone across the table: 'The OpenEd Daily.' I hope we can get coffee again soon."

**Why it failed:**
- Too long and narrative-heavy - requires commitment to read through
- Abstract/poetic messaging vs. concrete benefits
- "Secret" framing creates curiosity gap but payoff (newsletter) feels anticlimactic
- No specific numbers or tangible outcomes
- Speaks to already-converted homeschoolers, not prospects considering the switch

### Img-TextHeavy-V3 (Copy) - $121.77 CPA
**Format:** Square image, white text on dark blue background with bright yellow circles/highlights
**Copy on creative:**
> "If your child is struggling in school, stop scrolling and start exploring 110+ free education resources from OpenEd. With interactive lessons and hands-on activities, OpenEd has everything you need to get your child excited to learn- all for free and easy to use. Click below to see them now."

**Why it failed:**
- **Exact same copy as V1** but different visual treatment
- Dark blue + yellow is busier and less native-feeling than black on white
- Bright highlights compete with the text for attention
- Looks more like a designed ad vs. V1's organic/native feel
- Proves that visual treatment matters as much as copy

## Key Insights

1. **MG2 is the winner** - Lowest cost per application at $4.25 with solid volume (142 results)

2. **Notes-style ads perform consistently well** - Notes3 delivered 747 applications at $5.33 each, making it the best high-volume performer

3. **Video TikTok responses are mixed** - "AsADad" version works ($5.10/app), but "Finland" and "SecretWeapon" versions underperform significantly

4. **Text-heavy images work** - Img-TextHeavy-V1 and V2 both deliver under $6/application

5. **Testimonials convert efficiently** - $5.85 per application with good CTR (1.19%)

6. **Avoid**: Vid-SecretWeapon and the Finland TikTok response - both have CPAs 2-4x higher than top performers

**Sweet spot for cost per application:** $4.25 - $6.50

Anything above $10/application should be evaluated for pausing or iteration.

## Creative Patterns That Work

### Format Patterns
- **Notes app format** (iPhone notes aesthetic) - native, lo-fi feel
- **MG (meme/graphic) formats** - especially MG2 with atmospheric video backgrounds
- **Text-heavy static images** - V1 and V2 variants, black on white
- **Testimonial compilations** - real parent photos + specific outcomes
- **TikTok response videos** - skeptic-turned-believer narrative works

### Messaging Patterns
- **Question hooks** addressing common barriers ("don't know where to start?")
- **Pattern interrupts** ("stop scrolling")
- **Specific numbers** (110+ resources, 15,000+ families, 3 min to read)
- **Transformation stories** (struggling â†’ thriving)
- **Low-friction CTAs** (free, easy, newsletter vs full commitment)
- **Relatable skepticism** ("as a dad who was super skeptical")

### Visual Patterns
- Lo-fi/native aesthetics outperform polished
- Text as primary visual element works
- Atmospheric background video with text overlay
- Clean, simple layouts (Canva-style for testimonials)

## Creative Patterns to Avoid

### Format Failures
- **Long narrative copy** - "Secret Weapon" storytelling feels weird and irrelevant, disconnected from prospect's actual concerns
- **Excessive visual styling** - Yellow highlights and underlines in V3 feel like corporate slop, not authentic content
- **Abstract/poetic messaging** - Metaphors about "coffee stains and confidence" don't hook or resonate

### Key Lesson: V1 vs V3 Comparison
The **exact same copy** performed 28x worse with a different visual treatment:
- V1 (black on white): $5.82/app, 1,278 results
- V3 (white on dark blue + highlights): $121.77/app, 4 results

**Takeaway:** Excessive styling destroys performance. The highlights and underlines in V3 are distracting, feel corporate, and undermine trust. Simple, clean visuals that don't scream "ad" dramatically outperform.
