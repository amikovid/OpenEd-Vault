Who It's Best For

  • Kinesthetic learners who need to physically manipulate objects to understand concepts
  • Students who've struggled with other math programs or have math anxiety
  • Visual learners who benefit from seeing math represented with colored blocks
  • Parents who want structure but need video support for teaching
  • Families with multiple children - manipulatives and instructor packs can be reused

Who It Might Not Fit

  • Advanced math students who need more challenge and problem-solving depth
  • Kids who crave variety - the mastery approach means spending extended time on one concept
  • Families on tight budgets - the upfront cost for manipulatives and materials adds up
  • Independent learners who don't want parent involvement - younger levels require supervision

What It Covers

Complete K-12 mathematics program: From fundamental arithmetic through calculus. The sequence is mastery-based, meaning students don't move forward until they've truly grasped each concept.

Teaching format: Video lessons (DVD or streaming), physical manipulatives (the famous colored blocks), student workbooks, and teacher manuals. The videos are calm and methodical - Steve Demme teaches like he's sitting at your kitchen table.

Special offerings: Optional "Stewardship" course for personal finance with biblical principles.

Pricing

  • Instruction Pack: $70-90 per level (teacher manual + videos)
  • Student Pack: ~$30 per student per level (workbook + tests)
  • Main Integer Block Kit: ~$80 one-time (used Primer through Delta)
  • Fraction Overlays: ~$50 for upper levels

Cost-saving tip: The manipulatives and instruction packs can be reused for multiple children. Look for used materials in homeschool resale groups.

Parent Involvement

Moderate involvement required. You'll need to preview the video lessons, facilitate hands-on work with the manipulatives, and check completed work. The good news: the videos do the heavy lifting on instruction. As kids get older, many can watch lessons and work more independently.

Common Concerns

Cost: Yes, it's an investment. But the materials are reusable, and many families find the video instruction saves them from hiring a tutor.

Pacing: Some kids find the mastery approach slow. If your child already "gets it," you can skip practice pages - the program is designed for this flexibility.

Not enough challenge: For math-talented kids, consider supplementing with problem-solving resources like Beast Academy or Art of Problem Solving.

Alternatives to Consider

  • Saxon Math — Traditional, spiral approach with constant review. Pair with Nicole the Math Lady for video instruction.
  • Beast Academy — For advanced learners who want challenge and depth, taught through comic books.
  • Teaching Textbooks — Video-based and self-paced, but less rigorous. Great for independent learners.
  • Singapore Math — Rigorous and conceptual, but requires more parent teaching ability.

The Bottom Line

Math-U-See works because it makes abstract math concrete. If your child has struggled with math, if they're a hands-on learner, or if you've tried other programs without success - this is worth trying. The upfront investment pays off when you see your child finally understand rather than just memorize.

It's not for everyone. If your child needs variety or craves challenge beyond calculation, look elsewhere. But for the right kid, Math-U-See can transform math from a daily battle into genuine confidence.
