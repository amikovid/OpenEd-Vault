# Segment Titles Reference

Write segment headlines for OpenEd Daily. Short, punchy, create information gaps.

---

## Constraints

- **1-6 words** (shorter is better)
- **ALL CAPS** in final output
- **Fit one line** of narrow email box
- **Create information gap** - reader must read to understand

---

## Patterns

### The Label
Names a phenomenon. Reader wants to know what it means.

- THE GETTING BY TRAP
- THE CREDENTIAL ILLUSION
- THE PERMISSION MYTH

### The Stat
Number creates intrigue. Reader wants context.

- 83% OF PARENTS AGREE
- 1.5 MILLION AND COUNTING
- THE 4-DAY SOLUTION

### The Object
Clear, direct. For tool recommendations.

- TOOL: CHOMPSAW
- TREND: MICROSCHOOL SURGE
- THOUGHT: HAPPY NOT HARVARD

### The Contrast
Tension between two things.

- SMALL SCHOOLS BIG IMPACT
- CREDENTIALS VS COMMUNITY
- THE OTHER KIND OF GAP

### The Counterintuitive
Challenges assumption without revealing answer.

- WHY GRADES FAIL
- LESS STRUCTURE MORE LEARNING
- WHEN QUITTING IS WINNING

### The Witty (Pirate Wires Style)
Wordplay, cultural reference. Family-friendly wit.

- SCHOOL'S OUT FOREVER
- THE UNSCHOOL OF THOUGHT
- HOME IS WHERE THE SCHOOL IS
- READ BETWEEN THE LINES

---

## Sticky Techniques

Make titles memorable. Combine for maximum effect.

**Alliteration:** PRACTICE PRODUCES PERMANENCE | SMALL SCHOOLS SUCCEED

**Contrast:** SMALL SCHOOLS BIG DIFFERENCE | LESS IS MORE

**Rhythm:** Two short balanced phrases | THE GAP THAT MATTERS

---

## Test Your Title

Ask: "Could someone who hasn't read the segment understand this?"

- If YES → Title reveals too much (no gap)
- If NO → Good - they need to read to understand
- If CONFUSED → Title is unclear (bad gap)
