## SEGMENT 1: THOUGHT - Joshua's Daughter Ella

**Source:** https://share.descript.com/view/Yyu4QESqzAu (clip from Isaac on Minimalists podcast)
**Full story:** Thursday's OpenEd podcast episode

### Angle Options (Pirate Wires style - punchy, witty, substance + take)

**A. The podcast tease (Recommended)**
When Isaac went on The Minimalists podcast, Joshua shared a story that stopped him cold. His daughter Ella was having stomach aches every morning. Anxiety about school. They tried everything within the system. Nothing worked. The full conversation drops Thursday - but here's the preview.

**B. The "clutter" reframe**
Joshua Fields Millburn didn't call it anxiety or a learning disability. He called it clutter. "The public school system was a type of clutter. It was getting in the way of her life, of her joy, of her peace." Different diagnosis. Different solution.

**C. The "Lord of the Flies" hook**
Joshua describes his daughter's school as "Lord of the Flies, but with a couple adults on the island." No desks. Recording studio. Bug room. Gardens. Kids 6-16 teaching each other. Ella's making films now. She's 12.

**D. The dyslexia twist**
Joshua didn't know he was dyslexic until age 40. Found out when his daughter was struggling to read and he took the test with her. "I'm like, well, yeah, reading is just really hard, right?" They're like: no.

**Recommended:** A - Teases Thursday, delivers value, creates anticipation.

## SEGMENT 2: TREND - Scrolling Is the New Smoking

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eYK1u8Vg5No (TEDxFargo)

### Angle Options

**A. The health stats angle**
2+ hours daily = 3x depression risk, 40% higher sleep problems. The data is brutal. But the insight is in how they respond: not "ban phones" but "treat them like cigarettes."

**B. The pleonexia angle (Recommended)**
Ancient Greek word for greed for things that can be counted. Views, likes, followers. The Minimalists reached half a billion people - then realized most views were 8/10ths of a second. What does that measure?

**C. The "milked cow" angle**
Social media is designed to "milk" your nervous system for emotional responses, leaving you energetically depleted for real relationships. Eye-tracking tech means the algorithm knows you better than you know yourself.

**D. The year-off experiment angle**
The Minimalists quit social media for a full year after their most successful year ever. What they learned: success in things that can be counted ≠ success that matters.

**Recommended:** B - Novel word (pleonexia), ties to deeper philosophy, gives readers something to think about beyond "screens bad."

## SEGMENT 3: TOOL - BePresent App

**Source:** https://www.bepresentapp.com/
**App Store:** https://apps.apple.com/us/app/bepresent-screen-time-control/id1644737181

### Angle Options (Pirate Wires style)

**A. The Gen Z antidote (Recommended)**
Jack and Charles Winston are Gen Z brothers. Jack worked at EY and DraftKings. Charles was a Googler. They grew up with smartphones in their hands. Watched their whole generation struggle. Tried every screen time app. Nothing worked. So they built their own. BePresent uses gamification - compete with friends, earn real rewards for staying off your phone. Makes it a game, not a punishment.

**B. The irony angle**
Two guys who came from Big Tech (Google, DraftKings) built an app to fight phone addiction. They know how the algorithms work. Now they're using gamification against itself.

**C. The founder story hook**
First generation to grow up with smartphones. First to build the antidote. Jack and Charles Winston watched their friends try for years to solve phone addiction. Nothing worked. BePresent is profitable. Raised $150K seed. And it turns screen time reduction into a game you actually want to play.

**Recommended:** A - Clean founder story, actionable, ties to the screens theme.
