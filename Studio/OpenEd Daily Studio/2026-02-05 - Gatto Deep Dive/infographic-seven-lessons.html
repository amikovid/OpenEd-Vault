John Taylor Gatto
The 7 Hidden Lessons
Every School Teaches
From "The Seven-Lesson Schoolteacher" - his NYC Teacher of the Year acceptance speech
1
Confusion
Everything taught out of context. History separated from literature, math from science - all divided by bells.
2
Class Position
Children sorted, ranked, and labeled. "I'm not a math person" becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy.
3
Indifference
When the bell rings, drop whatever you're doing. This teaches kids not to care too deeply about anything.
4
Emotional Dependency
Stars, red checks, smiles, frowns. Children become dependent on external validation.
5
Intellectual Dependency
Wait to be told what to think. Good students wait for the teacher to tell them what to study.
6
Provisional Self-Esteem
Your worth is determined by grades. Self-respect depends on expert opinion.
7
Surveillance
Constant monitoring normalized. No private time, no private space. Always being watched.
