Reference library of high-performing OpenEd content patterns. Use these as templates when generating new content.

## One-Liners (Text Heavy / Dude-With-Sign Style)

Short, punchy statements that reframe conventional education thinking. Work across all platforms.

### Top Performers

**"Your kid isn't 'bad at school' - school is bad at your kid."**
- Pattern: Flip the blame
- Why it works: Validates parent frustration, reframes the problem

**"We accept that kids walk, read, and talk at different ages - but expect them to learn algebra on the exact same day?"**
- Pattern: Obvious contradiction
- Why it works: Uses familiar truth to expose absurdity

**"Your child's obsession isn't the problem. It's their competitive advantage."**
- Pattern: Reframe negative as positive
- Why it works: Gives permission, relieves guilt

**"Imagine a generation who never had to suppress their curiosity to fit into a system..."**
- Pattern: Future vision
- Why it works: Aspirational, implies action

### More Examples

- "The most underrated skill in education? Knowing when to step back."
- "Four walls don't make a classroom. A curious mind does."
- "Don't teach kids what to think. Teach them how to learn."
- "The goal isn't to keep up with the class. It's to keep up with your kid."
- "Education isn't something done TO children. It's something designed FOR them."

### One-Liner Patterns

1. **Flip the blame**: Take what's blamed on the child, attribute to system
2. **Obvious contradiction**: Juxtapose accepted norm with absurd outcome
3. **Permission slip**: Give parents permission to do what they already want
4. **Future vision**: Paint picture of what's possible
5. **Redefine the goal**: Shift success metric from conventional to meaningful

## Daily Newsletter (Thought-Trend-Tool)

### Structure

```
Greeting

[Hook: Usually references a trend or provocative statement]

THOUGHT: [Philosophical/mindset piece]
TREND: [Current events/research]
TOOL: [Actionable resource or recommendation]

# [Thought Title]
[2-3 paragraphs with one core insight]

# [Trend Title]  
[2-3 paragraphs on what's happening]

# [Tool Title]
[Resource recommendation with clear CTA]

# [Meme/Tweet of the Day]
[Embedded humor or quotable tweet]
```

### Example: "The 6-Hour School Day is a Lie"

**Hook:** "A reclusive billionaire just proved it. PLUS: Why every textbook you own is now worthless."

**Thought - "Learning Happens at the Limit"**
- Core insight: Real growth at personal edge, not comfortable middle
- Quotable: "The key to every kid's happiness is high standards combined with high support."

**Trend - "Your Textbook is a Relic"**
- Data point: "Students complete grade levels in 20-40 hours instead of 180+ classroom hours"
- Source: Alpha School's Incept platform

**Tool - "The Time-Back Machine"**
- Recommendation: Alpha Anywhere platform
- Value prop: "2 hours of efficient learning followed by 4 hours of freedom"

### Example: "Einstein had a tutor?"

**Hook:** "Why 'experts' tell parents their kids should slow down - and what Einstein's tutor teaches us."

**Thought - "Einstein Had a Tutor"**
- Core insight: Genius isn't isolated - it requires mentorship
- Quotable: "Access isn't engagement. What's missing isn't information - it's the Max Talmuds."

**Trend - "High-Impact Tutoring"**
- Data point: "The average tutored student was above 98% of students in the control class."
- Source: The 74 Million / Liz Cohen research

**Tool - "What to Do With Genius"**
- Recommendation: Brilliant.org
- Value prop: Self-paced STEM for advanced learners, $25/month

### Newsletter Headline Patterns

1. **Question that challenges**: "Einstein had a tutor?"
2. **Provocative claim**: "The 6-Hour School Day is a Lie"
3. **Trend callout**: "Why 15 million students are in microschools now"
4. **Personal story**: "3 kids, 3 different schools, 1 surprising lesson"
5. **Contrarian take**: "Why bored kids are winning"

## Podcast Blog Post Pattern

### Structure

Blog posts derived from podcast episodes follow this pattern:

1. **Hook paragraph** - Story moment from episode
2. **Guest intro** - Who they are, why they matter
3. **3-5 Key Insights** - Pulled from transcript with headers
4. **Practical takeaway** - What parents can do
5. **CTA** - Listen to full episode

### Example Headlines from Podcasts

- "She Did Everything Right - Her Daughter Still Couldn't Read"
- "Why Your ADHD Kid Won't Listen - It's Not What You Think"
- "The Etsy Effect: How Not to Kill Your Child's Passions"
- "What Happens When a Teacher Tells Teens to Stop Going to School"
- "He Sold His House to Homeschool in a 300-Square-Foot RV"

### Podcast Headline Patterns

1. **Paradox**: Did right thing, wrong outcome
2. **Reframe common belief**: It's not what you think
3. **Named concept**: The Etsy Effect, The Diploma Dilemma
4. **Provocative action**: Told teens to stop going to school
5. **Extreme story**: Sold house, 300-sq-ft RV

## Short-Form Video Hooks

From Andrew Muto methodology (see ANDREW-MUTO-AUDIT.md for full context):

### Hook Categories

1. **Challenge conventional wisdom**: "School teaches kids to hate learning"
2. **Specific + unexpected**: "My 8-year-old runs a business"
3. **Pattern interrupt**: Start mid-action, no intro
4. **Emotional truth**: "I was terrified to pull my kid out"
5. **Data shock**: "15 million kids aren't in traditional school"

### First 3 Seconds Checklist

- [ ] No "Hey guys" or intro fluff
- [ ] Visual movement or change
- [ ] Statement that demands resolution
- [ ] Would pass McDonald's Test (truck driver understands it)

## Platform-Specific Notes

### LinkedIn
- Longer text posts perform well
- Professional angle: "What employers actually want"
- Carousel format for educational content
- Tag industry experts when relevant

### Instagram
- Reels dominate reach
- Text overlay on B-roll works
- Carousel for educational content
- Stories for behind-the-scenes

### X (Twitter)
- Thread format for deep dives
- Quote tweets for engagement
- Provocative one-liners
- Embed screenshots of interesting data

### TikTok
- Raw, unpolished preferred
- Educational content growing
- Duets/Stitches for reach
- Trending sounds optional but helpful
