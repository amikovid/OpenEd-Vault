## YouTube Description

Dr. Claire Honeycutt is a neuroscientist, researcher, and homeschool parent who recommends two philosophically opposed books - Well-Trained Mind and Free to Learn - and says you need both. In this second conversation with Ela, Claire walks through exactly how she built a home where Shakespeare is a breakfast puzzle, the TV isn't the center of the room, and a pool table replaced the dining table. She explains why sensitive developmental windows matter (kids ages 6-9 are learning 50 words a day), how she introduces rigor without killing joy, and why she thinks kids need to learn to think before they learn to prompt. If you missed Part 1, where Claire talks about leaving her tenured neuroscience career to homeschool, go watch that first: [LINK TO PART 1]
