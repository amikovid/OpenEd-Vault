## Content Playbooks

### State Guide Template

**Target length:** 2,000-2,500 words

**Structure:**
1. **Quick Facts Table** - Compulsory ages, notification requirements, assessment rules
2. **How to Start** - Step-by-step for traditional homeschool vs. OpenEd accredited path
3. **State Laws Breakdown** - What's required, what's optional
4. **Funding Options** - ESAs, OpenEd reimbursement, tax credits
5. **Curriculum Recommendations** - Linked to grade level guides
6. **Local Resources** - Co-ops, microschools, field trip destinations
7. **FAQ Section** - 5-7 common questions with schema markup
8. **CTA** - Enrollment or info session

**SEO Requirements:**
- Title: "How to Homeschool in [State]: Complete Guide [Year]"
- H1 can be creative
- Include "[state] homeschool laws" and "[state] homeschool requirements" naturally
- FAQ schema for all questions

---

### Grade Level Guide Template

**Target length:** 1,500-2,000 words

**Structure:**
1. **Grade Overview** - Developmental context, what kids this age need
2. **OpenEd Recommended Stack** - Math, Reading, Language Arts, Science, History, Extras
3. **Runner-Up Options** - Alternative picks for each subject
4. **Free Resources** - Quality free options for budget-conscious families
5. **Sample Schedule** - What a day/week might look like
6. **FAQ Section** - Common questions for this grade

**SEO Requirements:**
- Title: "[Grade] Homeschool Curriculum: What Actually Works [Year]"
- Include grade-specific long-tail keywords
- Link to subject-specific deep dives

---No just create new pages 

### Thinker Profile Template

**Target length:** 1,500-2,000 words

**Structure:**
1. **Opening Hook** - Compelling quote or story that captures their essence
2. **Who They Were/Are** - Brief biography
3. **Core Ideas** - 3-5 key concepts explained accessibly
4. **Why It Matters Today** - Relevance to modern homeschooling
5. **Criticism & Counterpoints** - Fair treatment of objections
6. **How to Apply This** - Practical takeaways for parents
7. **Further Reading** - Books, articles, videos

**SEO Requirements:**
- Title: "[Name]: [Compelling angle or key idea]"
- Include "[name] education" and "[name] homeschool" naturally
- Link to related pedagogy hubs

---

### Comparison (Vs.) Page Template

**Target length:** 2,000-2,500 words

**Structure:**
1. **TL;DR** - Quick recommendation for different family types
2. **Comparison Table** - Side-by-side features, philosophy, cost, ages
3. **[Option A] Explained** - Deep dive on first option
4. **[Option B] Explained** - Deep dive on second option
5. **Key Differences** - What really sets them apart
6. **Who Should Choose What** - Decision framework by family situation
7. **Can You Combine Them?** - Hybrid approaches
8. **FAQ Section** - Common comparison questions

**SEO Requirements:**
- Title: "[A] vs [B]: Which Is Right for Your Family? [Year]"
- Include both terms naturally throughout
- FAQ schema

---

### Tool Review Template

**Target length:** 1,500-2,000 words

**Structure:**
1. **Quick Verdict** - Rating, best for, price
2. **What It Is** - Overview for someone who's never heard of it
3. **How It Works** - Walkthrough of the experience
4. **What We Like** - Genuine positives
5. **What Could Be Better** - Honest limitations
6. **Pricing** - Current pricing breakdown
7. **Alternatives** - Other options to consider
8. **Who It's Best For** - Specific family/learner profiles
9. **OpenEd Teacher Take** - Quote from our teachers

**SEO Requirements:**
- Title: "[Tool] Review [Year]: Is It Worth It?"
- Include "[tool] review" and "[tool] vs [alternative]" naturally
- Product schema if applicable

## Distribution Strategy

Each content type gets a specific social distribution plan.

### State Guides → Social Strategy

**Primary platforms:** Facebook (local groups), LinkedIn (professional credibility)

**Facebook distribution:**
- Post to state-specific homeschool groups (manually, respect group rules)
- OpenEd page post with state tag
- Boost consideration for new state launches

**LinkedIn distribution:**
- Template: Authority + practical value
- Example: "We just published the most comprehensive guide to homeschooling in Nevada. Here's what surprised us about the laws there..."
- Tag any local education advocates

**X/Twitter distribution:**
- Thread format: 5-7 key facts from the guide
- Tag state homeschool associations if they exist

**Email:**
- Segment by state, send relevant guide to families in that state

---

### Grade Level Guides → Social Strategy

**Primary platforms:** Facebook (parent communities), Pinterest (curriculum planning)

**Facebook distribution:**
- Post to grade-specific homeschool groups
- "What curriculum are you using for [grade]?" engagement posts linking to guide

**Pinterest distribution:**
- Create pin with curriculum stack visual
- Title: "[Grade] Homeschool Curriculum Checklist"
- Link to full guide

**Instagram distribution:**
- Carousel: "5 must-haves for [grade] homeschool"
- Save-worthy, shareable format

**Email:**
- Back-to-school timing (July-August)
- Mid-year "is this working?" timing (January)

---

### Thinkers Series → Social Strategy

**Primary platforms:** LinkedIn (thought leadership), X/Twitter (intellectual discourse)

**LinkedIn distribution:**
- Template: Contrarian insight + relevance today
- Example: "John Taylor Gatto won NYC Teacher of the Year, then quit and called schools 'psychological murder.' Here's what he saw that we're still ignoring..."
- Long-form post, link in comments

**X/Twitter distribution:**
- Thread format: Key ideas as provocative statements
- Quote graphics from the thinker
- Tag education thought leaders for engagement

**Newsletter:**
- Feature in OpenEd Daily "Thought" section
- Deep link to full profile

**Podcast:**
- Reference in relevant episodes
- Consider dedicated "Thinkers" mini-series

---

### Comparison (Vs.) Pages → Social Strategy

**Primary platforms:** All (high search intent = high share intent)

**Facebook distribution:**
- Post to method-specific groups (Montessori groups, Charlotte Mason groups)
- Poll format: "Which do you prefer: [A] or [B]?" then link to comparison

**LinkedIn distribution:**
- Template: "The difference isn't what you think"
- Example: "Everyone asks 'Waldorf or Montessori?' But that's the wrong question. Here's what actually matters..."

**X/Twitter distribution:**
- Hot take format: "[A] vs [B] comes down to one thing: [insight]"
- Thread with key differences

**Pinterest distribution:**
- Comparison infographic pin
- High save potential for curriculum planners

**Instagram distribution:**
- Carousel comparing key features
- Swipe-through decision tree

---

### Tool Reviews → Social Strategy

**Primary platforms:** Facebook (practical recommendations), YouTube (demo potential)

**Facebook distribution:**
- Post to curriculum review groups
- "Has anyone tried [Tool]?" engagement posts

**YouTube consideration:**
- Walkthrough videos for high-volume tools
- Screen recording + voiceover format

**Instagram distribution:**
- Reel: Quick tool demo or "3 things I wish I knew about [Tool]"

**Email:**
- "Tool of the Week" segment in newsletter
- Segment by subject interest (math tools to math-struggling families)
