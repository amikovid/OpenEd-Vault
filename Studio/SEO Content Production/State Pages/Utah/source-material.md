## Legal Requirements (HSLDA)

### At a Glance
| Requirement | Utah |
|-------------|------|
| Compulsory Ages | 6–18 |
| Notification Required | Yes (one-time notice of intent) |
| Teacher Qualifications | None |
| State Mandated Subjects | None |
| Assessment Requirements | None |
| Immunization Requirements | None |
| Regulation Level | Low |

### How to Comply (Utah Code Ann. § 53G-6-204)

**Step 1: File a Notice of Intent**
- One-time filing with local school board for each child
- Updated May 7, 2025: replaced previous affidavit requirement
- After filing, receive "excuse certificate" within 30 days
- Do NOT need to refile as long as you continue homeschooling

**Step 2: Provide Instruction**
- No required days of instruction
- No required subjects
- Parent solely responsible for selection of materials/textbooks

### Public School Access (Utah Code § 53G-6-702, § 53G-6-703)
- Homeschool students eligible for extracurricular activities at public schools
- Same eligibility standards as enrolled students
- Districts cannot impose additional requirements on homeschoolers
- "Dual enrollment" option: enroll part-time in public school while homeschooling
- Can participate in academic activities, sports, and programs

## Funding Options

### Utah Fits All Scholarship (ESA)
- State-funded education savings account
- Available to Utah families
- Can be used for curriculum, tutoring, educational services
- Separate from OpenEd - families can use both

### OpenEd Utah
- Free through partnership with public schools
- Access to educational resources at no cost
- Marketplace with 50+ vendors
- Direct ordering from Amazon/Rainbow Resource
- Learning logs, education plan builder

**Note:** OpenEd families are students of accredited program, not "homeschoolers" under state law. Different (simpler) requirements apply - OpenEd handles compliance.

## Local Resources (From OpenEd Content)

### Nature & Outdoor Programs

**Nature Kids Connect** (naturekidsconnect.com)
- Nature co-op with weekly Friday meetups
- Programs: OWLS (Provo/Orem), Coyotes (Salt Lake County), Teen Class
- Age range: 8+ for main programs, 11+ for Teen Class
- Schedule: Weekly Friday meetups, 10:30 am to 2:30 pm
- Activities: Hiking, group games, nature observation, outdoor skills, field trips
- Unique offerings: Backpacking trips, sunrise hikes, ice fishing, river rafting
- Student quote: "Technology can be a vortex. It can suck you in and you can get addicted to it."

**Breakout School** (breakoutschool.org)
- 80-90% time spent outdoors
- Specifically designed for students with autism and ADHD
- Students who struggled in traditional school are thriving

### Microschools & Learning Studios

**Apogee Utah** (apogeeutah.com)
- Location: South Jordan, Utah
- Founded by Peggy Lowe (12-year veteran teacher)
- Philosophy: "Guides" not teachers - student-directed learning
- Students manage own schedules
- Self-regulation focus
- Quote from founder: "There were times my fellow guide and I would just look at each other and whisper 'shh... don't say anything. Let them figure it out.'"

**Orchard STEM School** (orchardstem.org)
- K-8 microschool in a working orchard
- Location: Santaquin, Utah
- STEM focus with hands-on agricultural learning
