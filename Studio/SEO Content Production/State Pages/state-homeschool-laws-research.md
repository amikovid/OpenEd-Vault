### UTAH

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Utah

| Requirement | Utah |
|-------------|------|
| Compulsory Ages | 6-18 |
| Notification Required | Yes - one-time Notice of Intent to local school board |
| Subjects Required | None mandated (core subjects encouraged) |
| Assessment Required | No |
| Teacher Qualifications | High school diploma or equivalent |
| Regulation Level | Low |

#### Notification/Registration

- File a **one-time notice of intent** with your local school board for each child when you start homeschooling
- As of May 2025, the affidavit requirement was replaced with a simpler notice of intent (Utah Code Ann. SS 53G-6-204)
- School board issues an "excuse certificate" within 30 days acknowledging your homeschool
- No need to refile as long as you continue homeschooling

#### Required Subjects

Utah does not require a specific curriculum. Parents are encouraged to cover language arts, mathematics, science, and social studies, but there is no mandate.

#### Assessment/Testing

No standardized testing or assessments required.

#### Instructional Requirements

180 instructional days per school year.

#### Teacher Qualifications

Parent must have a high school diploma or equivalent.

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

Homeschooled students may participate in sports, activities, and classes at local public schools. Many districts offer hybrid access including sports, extracurriculars, and some performance arts programs.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. No state certification of homeschool diplomas.

#### Key Organizations

- **Utah Home Education Association (UHEA)** - uhea.org - Non-profit serving Utah homeschoolers for 35+ years. Annual convention, local group directory, legislative advocacy.
- **Utah Christian Home School Association (UTCH)** - utch.org - Since 1991. Christian-focused. Support groups, co-ops, annual convention, legislative representation.
- **U-HOPE Coalition** - Coalition of Utah home education organizations for unified legislative advocacy.

#### ESA/School Choice

**Utah Fits All Scholarship Program**
- $8,000 per student (ages 5-11: $4,000; ages 12-18: $6,000) for 2025-2026
- Covers curriculum, tutoring, extracurriculars, testing fees, therapies
- 2025 legislation capped extracurricular spending at 20% of ESA value
- **STATUS:** Ruled unconstitutional spring 2025, but allowed to continue for 2025-2026 while under review
- Approved uses: curriculum, tutoring, therapies (OT, speech, behavioral), after-school programs, extracurriculars, instrument rental, AP/certification exams, assessments

### INDIANA

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Indiana

| Requirement | Indiana |
|-------------|---------|
| Compulsory Ages | 7-18 |
| Notification Required | No (voluntary only) |
| Subjects Required | English language equivalent to public school |
| Assessment Required | No |
| Teacher Qualifications | None |
| Regulation Level | Low |

#### Notification/Registration

- **No notification required** under state law
- Indiana DOE offers an online enrollment form, but completion is **voluntary** - public school officials sometimes suggest it is required, but it is not
- If withdrawing from public school, notify the school of your withdrawal
- Homeschools are classified as **non-accredited private schools**

#### Required Subjects

Must teach in English and provide instruction "equivalent to that given in public schools." No specific subject list, but English language instruction is mandatory.

#### Assessment/Testing

No mandatory testing or evaluation.

#### Instructional Requirements

Must operate for the same number of days as public schools in your district (typically 180 days). Attendance records required.

#### Teacher Qualifications

None. No teaching license, degree, or certification required.

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

No state law guarantees homeschool access to public school sports. Access depends on local school district policy. The Indiana High School Athletic Association (IHSAA) does not have a statewide policy allowing homeschool participation.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. Indiana does not certify homeschool diplomas, but colleges and employers generally accept parent-issued diplomas with transcripts.

#### Key Organizations

- **Indiana Association of Home Educators (IAHE)** - iahe.net - Founded 1983. Annual convention, magazine ("Homeschool Indiana"), podcast, 17 regional representatives, legislative advocacy through sister org IAHE Action.

#### ESA/School Choice

- **Indiana Choice Scholarship (Voucher):** Universal eligibility as of 2025, but for **private school enrollment only** - cannot be used for homeschooling
- **Education Scholarship Account (ESA):** For students with special needs and siblings. Up to $20,000 for disabled students, $8,000 for siblings.
- **Tax deduction** available for homeschool expenses
- **HB 1636** (proposed): Would create "dynamic choice scholarships" for broader educational expenses beyond tuition

### KANSAS

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Kansas

| Requirement | Kansas |
|-------------|-------|
| Compulsory Ages | 7-18 (can end at 16-17 with consent) |
| Notification Required | Yes - register as non-accredited private school with KSBE |
| Subjects Required | None specified (refer to state standards) |
| Assessment Required | Periodic testing (unspecified method/frequency) |
| Teacher Qualifications | "Competent instructor" (not defined) |
| Regulation Level | Low |

#### Notification/Registration

- Register as a **Non-Accredited Private School** with the Kansas State Board of Education
- Free registration form requiring: school name/address, custodian of records name/address
- Registration is simple and serves primarily as a record of your homeschool's existence

#### Required Subjects

No specific subject requirements. Kansas Curricular Standards are available as a reference but not mandated for homeschoolers.

#### Assessment/Testing

- Exempt from **state testing** requirements
- Law requires "instruction that is planned and scheduled with periodic testing" - but no specification on method, frequency, or reporting
- Parents can choose standardized tests, portfolio reviews, or project-based assessments
- No requirement to share results with anyone

#### Instructional Requirements

186 days of not less than 6 hours per day (1,116 hours for grades 1-11).

#### Teacher Qualifications

Must be a "competent instructor" - Kansas law does not define what this means and does not require a teaching license.

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

**Yes.** A 2023 law allows homeschool students to participate in all KSHSAA activities through their resident school district. Students must demonstrate academic eligibility through a parent/teacher affidavit or transcript showing satisfactory progress.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. No state certification.

#### Key Organizations

- **Midwest Parent Educators (MPE)** - midwesthomeschoolers.org - Formerly Christian Home Educators Confederation of Kansas. Co-ops, athletics, enrichment programs.
- **Kansas Home Educators** - kshomeeducators.com - Statewide information and support.

#### ESA/School Choice

No ESA, voucher, or tax credit program for homeschoolers. School choice options limited to public schools, charters, magnets, and private schools.

### IOWA

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Iowa

| Requirement | Iowa |
|-------------|------|
| Compulsory Ages | 6-16 |
| Notification Required | Varies by option (IPI: none; CPI: annual notice) |
| Subjects Required | Math, science, reading/LA, social studies (under CPI options) |
| Assessment Required | Varies by option (optional for many) |
| Teacher Qualifications | None |
| Regulation Level | Low to Moderate (depending on option chosen) |

#### Notification/Registration

Iowa offers **three homeschool pathways** with different requirements:

1. **Independent Private Instruction (IPI):** No notification, no assessment, no parent qualifications, no instruction time requirements. Must teach math, science, reading/LA, and social studies. *Lowest regulation option.*

2. **Competent Private Instruction (CPI) Option 1:** Annual notice required. Homeschool under supervision of a certified teacher who monitors progress. Must teach required subjects.

3. **Competent Private Instruction (CPI) Option 2:** 148 days of instruction required. Students must make adequate progress. Notification and assessment are optional unless seeking dual enrollment or extracurricular access.

#### Required Subjects

Under all options: math, science, reading and language arts, and social studies.

#### Assessment/Testing

- **IPI:** No assessment required
- **CPI Option 1:** Certified teacher monitors and records progress
- **CPI Option 2:** Assessment (standardized test or portfolio review) is optional UNLESS seeking dual enrollment or extracurricular access
- Assessments reported to local school district only if participating in public school services

#### Instructional Requirements

CPI requires at least 148 days (37 days per quarter). IPI has no instruction time requirement.

#### Teacher Qualifications

None required under any option.

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

Iowa public schools must offer **dual enrollment** to homeschooled students. Through dual enrollment, homeschoolers can access classes, sports, and activities. Families may need to pay applicable fees. 2025 legislation expanded private school student access to public school sports.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. No state certification.

#### Key Organizations

- **Homeschool Iowa** - homeschooliowa.org - Statewide organization. Annual conference, legislative updates, community resources.
- **Iowa Alliance for Choice in Education (Iowa ACE)** - iowaace.org - School choice advocacy.

#### ESA/School Choice

**Students First ESA:**
- **$7,988 per student** for 2025-2026 (half fall, half spring)
- Universal eligibility starting 2025-2026
- **For accredited nonpublic school enrollment only** - homeschoolers cannot use ESA funds unless they enroll in an accredited private school
- Three school choice programs total: ESA, tax-credit scholarship, individual tax credit

### MONTANA

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Montana

| Requirement | Montana |
|-------------|---------|
| Compulsory Ages | 7-16 |
| Notification Required | Yes - annual to County Superintendent |
| Subjects Required | Basic: math, reading, science |
| Assessment Required | No |
| Teacher Qualifications | None |
| Regulation Level | Low |

#### Notification/Registration

- **Annual notification** to the County Superintendent of Schools for each child ages 7-16
- Can be done in person, by letter, or phone call (writing recommended)
- Attendance records only reported to County Superintendent if requested
- As of May 2025 (HB778): Immunization records no longer required upon request

#### Required Subjects

Must provide an "organized and sequential educational program" in basic subjects: math, reading, and science.

#### Assessment/Testing

No mandatory testing or evaluation.

#### Instructional Hours

- Grades 1-3: 720 hours
- Grades 4-12: 1,080 hours
- Kindergarten: 260 hours (half-day) or 720 hours (full-day)
- School year runs July 1 to June 30

#### Teacher Qualifications

None. No degree, credential, or certification required.

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

Homeschool students can enroll part-time in public schools (2023 law), enabling access to sports and activities. Some communities have dedicated homeschool athletic programs (e.g., Gallatin Valley Cougars).

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. For a more formal credential, students can take the HiSET exam (recognized as HS diploma equivalency by the State of Montana).

#### Key Organizations

- **Montana Coalition of Home Educators (MCHE)** - mtche.org - Founded 1988. Statewide network of support groups, legal information, getting started resources, legislative advocacy.
- **Homeschool Montana** - homeschoolmt.com - Resources and community.

#### ESA/School Choice

- **Special Needs Equal Opportunity ESA:** For students with disabilities. $5,000-$8,000 per family for tuition, tutoring, therapy, and educational materials.
- **Tax-Credit Scholarship:** $6 million aggregate in 2025; $7.2 million in 2026. Donations to student scholarship organizations. Can assist with homeschool expenses through scholarship organizations.
- **Federal Tax Credit (2027):** Up to $1,700 nonrefundable federal credit for contributions to approved SGOs.

### MINNESOTA

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Minnesota

| Requirement | Minnesota |
|-------------|-----------|
| Compulsory Ages | 7-17 |
| Notification Required | Yes - annual Notice of Intent by October 1 |
| Subjects Required | Yes - 12 subjects |
| Assessment Required | Yes - annual standardized test |
| Teacher Qualifications | None (for own children) |
| Regulation Level | Moderate |

#### Notification/Registration

- **Annual Notice of Intent** to your local school district superintendent by **October 1**
- If beginning mid-year, file at least **15 days before** withdrawing from school
- Must include basic family and student information

#### Required Subjects

Minnesota requires instruction in **12 subjects:**
1. Reading
2. Writing
3. Literature
4. Fine arts
5. Math
6. Science
7. History
8. Geography
9. Economics
10. Government and citizenship
11. Health
12. Physical education

Note: How often these are taught and at what age/grade level are NOT defined. Families are not required to teach all subjects every year.

#### Assessment/Testing

- **Annual standardized testing** required for students ages 7-17 in unaccredited homeschools
- Must be a nationally normed achievement test
- If student scores at or below the **30th percentile**, evaluation for a learning disability is required
- Accredited homeschools are exempt from testing requirement
- Parents keep records of test results

#### Instructional Requirements

Record-keeping required showing required subjects are being taught and assessments are being completed.

#### Teacher Qualifications

- **Parents teaching their own children:** No qualifications required
- **Non-parent instructors:** Must hold a MN teaching license, baccalaureate degree, be supervised by a licensed teacher, or have passed a teacher competency exam

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

Minnesota allows homeschoolers access to public school sports and activities, though specifics may vary by district.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. Neither the Minnesota Department of Education nor local school districts certify or validate any nonpublic or homeschool diploma or transcript.

#### Key Organizations

- **Minnesota Association of Christian Home Educators (MACHE)** - homeschoolminnesota.org / mache.org - Founded 1983. Annual convention, bi-weekly newsletter, legislative monitoring, support groups and co-ops directory.
- **HOPE Co-op** and other local cooperatives available statewide.

#### ESA/School Choice

- **Individual tax credit and tax deduction** for educational expenses (existing programs)
- **ESA bill (S.F. 1525):** Proposed but not yet enacted. Would fund K-12 education expenses including tutoring, curriculum, therapy, and private school tuition.
- No ESA or voucher currently available for homeschoolers.

### ARKANSAS

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Arkansas

| Requirement | Arkansas |
|-------------|---------|
| Compulsory Ages | 5-17 |
| Notification Required | Yes - annual NOI to district superintendent by August 15 |
| Subjects Required | None mandated |
| Assessment Required | No (since 2015) |
| Teacher Qualifications | None |
| Regulation Level | Low |

#### Notification/Registration

- **Annual Notice of Intent (NOI)** to local school district superintendent
- Deadline: **August 15** (or December 15 for spring-semester starts)
- NOI must include: student name, DOB, grade level, last school attended, homeschool location, basic core curriculum overview, proposed schedule, parent-teacher qualifications
- 2025-2026 NOI available starting **June 1, 2025**

#### Required Subjects

No mandated subjects, assessments, or record-keeping requirements (since 2015 legislative changes).

#### Assessment/Testing

**None required.** Arkansas removed year-end testing requirements for homeschoolers in 2015. Testing is available voluntarily through public schools. If participating in EFA program, assessment after March 1 is required.

#### Instructional Requirements

Same attendance expectations as public/private school students, but with flexibility in structuring the academic calendar.

#### Teacher Qualifications

None. No degree, certification, or educational requirements for parents.

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

**Yes - "Tim Tebow" Law (since 2013).** Homeschooled students can try out for and participate in extracurricular activities at their local public school. Students must demonstrate academic eligibility and maintain participation standards.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. Widely accepted by colleges and employers with supporting transcript.

#### Key Organizations

- **Arkansas Homeschool** - arkansashomeschool.org - Information on laws, extracurricular access, getting started.
- **Arkansas Homeschool Freedom** - arkansashomeschoolfreedom.com - Legislative advocacy and family resources.
- **School Choice Arkansas** - schoolchoicear.org - EFA program support.

#### ESA/School Choice

**Education Freedom Account (EFA) - via LEARNS Act (2023):**
- **$6,864 per student per year** ($1,716 quarterly) for 2025-2026
- Succeed Scholarship recipients: $7,627
- **Universal eligibility** - every K-12 student in Arkansas
- Homeschoolers ARE eligible
- Funds disbursed via ClassWallet
- Approved expenses: curriculum, tutoring, testing, therapy, technology
- **Act 920 (2025):** Extracurricular spending capped at 25% of annual EFA funding
- If using EFA, students must be assessed after March 1 of the school year and share results with ADE
- Applications open through August 15
- Home school enrollment grew 5.4% in 2024-2025

### NEVADA

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Nevada

| Requirement | Nevada |
|-------------|--------|
| Compulsory Ages | 6-18 |
| Notification Required | Yes - NOI to local superintendent |
| Subjects Required | English, math, science, social studies |
| Assessment Required | No |
| Teacher Qualifications | None |
| Regulation Level | Low |

#### Notification/Registration

- File **written Notice of Intent** with the superintendent of the school district where the child resides
- Within **10 days** of withdrawing from public school, OR within **30 days** of establishing residency in the state
- Use the form developed by the Department of Education
- School district provides written acknowledgment as proof of compliance with compulsory attendance
- Compulsory age changed from 7 to 6 in 2023 (AB65)

#### Required Subjects

Must teach: English (reading, writing, composition), math, science, social studies (economics, government, history, geography).

#### Assessment/Testing

No mandatory testing or assessment.

#### Instructional Requirements

No instruction time requirements, no bookkeeping requirements.

#### Teacher Qualifications

None. No certification, degree, or educational requirements.

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

**Yes - state law.** Homeschooled students must be allowed to participate in interscholastic activities and events (including sports) through the public school in their attendance zone. A notice of intent to participate must be filed with the school district for the current year.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. No state certification.

#### Key Organizations

- **Nevada Homeschool Network** - nevadahomeschoolnetwork.com - Quick start guides, legal information, education options, community resources.

#### ESA/School Choice

- **ESA (passed 2015, blocked 2016):** First universal ESA in the nation, but **never funded**. Law technically exists but no families receive funds. Repealed operationally in 2019.
- **Opportunity Scholarship:** Up to **$10,094 per student** for 2025-2026 - available for private school, not homeschooling
- **2025 legislation:** Open enrollment system for inter/intra-district public school transfers
- **Bottom line:** No direct state financial support currently available for homeschoolers in Nevada

### NEW MEXICO

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in New Mexico

| Requirement | New Mexico |
|-------------|------------|
| Compulsory Ages | 5-18 |
| Notification Required | Yes - annual Letter of Intent to NMPED by August 1 |
| Subjects Required | Reading, language arts, math, social studies, science |
| Assessment Required | No |
| Teacher Qualifications | High school diploma or GED |
| Regulation Level | Low |

#### Notification/Registration

- **Annual Letter of Intent** to the New Mexico Public Education Department (NMPED)
- Due by **August 1**, or within 30 days of starting homeschool
- Submitted through **online portal**
- File annually through the portal

#### Required Subjects

Reading, language arts, math, social studies, and science.

#### Assessment/Testing

No testing requirements for homeschooled students.

#### Instructional Requirements

Must provide instruction for the same length of time as local public schools - generally **180 days or about 1,140 hours per year.**

#### Teacher Qualifications

Teaching parent must have a **high school diploma or equivalent (GED).**

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

**Yes - state law.** New Mexico law requires local districts to allow homeschool students to participate in extracurricular activities, including athletics, if the student meets eligibility requirements. Students must maintain a **2.0 GPA**, complete a physical exam, and have a **STARS ID.** Can register for **up to 3 activities.**

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. Acceptance varies.

#### Immunization Records

Must keep immunization records or file a waiver (Form 454).

#### Key Organizations

- **Christian Association of Parent Educators (CAPE-NM)** - cape-nm.org - 30+ years. Annual convention in Albuquerque, legislative advocacy ("CAPE at the Capitol"), Fall Family Retreat, curriculum vendors.
- Various local co-ops and support groups statewide.

#### ESA/School Choice

- **No ESA or voucher program** for homeschoolers
- **No direct public funding** for any nonpublic schooling option
- **"The Education Plan" 529:** 100% deductible from state taxable income with **no cap** - one of only four states with unlimited 529 deductions. This is the most significant financial advantage for NM homeschoolers.

### WYOMING

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Wyoming

| Requirement | Wyoming |
|-------------|---------|
| Compulsory Ages | 7-16 |
| Notification Required | No (as of July 1, 2025 - Homeschool Freedom Act) |
| Subjects Required | Reading, writing, math, civics, history, literature, science |
| Assessment Required | No |
| Teacher Qualifications | None |
| Regulation Level | Low (recently reduced) |

#### Notification/Registration

**Major 2025 change:** The **Homeschool Freedom Act (HB 46)**, signed February 27, 2025, effective July 1, 2025:
- **Eliminates** the requirement to submit a Notice of Intent
- **Eliminates** the requirement to submit curriculum to local school districts
- If withdrawing a child from public school, a **one-time written notice** to the district is still needed
- Passed with bipartisan support (28-2 in Senate)

#### Required Subjects

Must provide a "sequentially progressive curriculum of fundamental instruction" in: reading, writing, mathematics, civics, history, literature, and science. However, curriculum no longer needs to be submitted to anyone.

#### Assessment/Testing

No standardized testing required.

#### Instructional Requirements

No specific instruction hour requirements defined in current law.

#### Teacher Qualifications

None. No degree or certification requirements.

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

**Yes.** Homeschool and ESA students have a right to participate in WHSAA-sanctioned high school activities, subject to the same cost/fee requirements as district-enrolled students.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. Note: NCAA has historically questioned homeschool diplomas from Wyoming (though districts can help resolve this due to prior curriculum requirements).

#### Key Organizations

- **Homeschool Wyoming** - homeschoolwy.org - Statewide organization. Membership has doubled in the past 5 school years (estimated 5,000-6,000 families).

#### ESA/School Choice

**Steamboat Legacy Scholarship Act (HB 199):**
- Up to **$7,000 per student**
- **Income-limited:** Household income must be at or below 250% of federal poverty level
- Available for 2025-2026 school year and beyond
- Homeschoolers can participate but face **additional requirements:** must teach specified subjects and submit to annual academic assessment
- Managed through registered providers/vendors
- Can be used for curriculum, tutoring, testing, and educational materials

### WASHINGTON

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Washington

| Requirement | Washington |
|-------------|------------|
| Compulsory Ages | 8-18 |
| Notification Required | Yes - annual Declaration of Intent by September 15 |
| Subjects Required | Yes - 11 subjects |
| Assessment Required | Yes - annual (test or certified teacher evaluation) |
| Teacher Qualifications | Yes - college credits, course, or supervision required |
| Regulation Level | Moderate to High |

#### Notification/Registration

- **Annual Declaration of Intent (DOI)** to the superintendent of the local school district
- Due by **September 15** or within two weeks of the start of any public school quarter/trimester/semester
- **New (SB 6261):** Parents of children ages **6 and 7** must file signed declarations if the child is not enrolled in school

#### Required Subjects

Must cover **11 subjects:**
1. Occupational education
2. Science
3. Math
4. Language
5. Social studies
6. History
7. Health
8. Reading
9. Writing
10. Spelling
11. Art and music appreciation

#### Assessment/Testing

- **Annual assessment** required
- Options: standardized testing OR evaluation by a certificated educator
- Test results are private - part of your homeschool record, not reported to the state

#### Instructional Requirements

At least **180 days per year** (approximately 1,000 total hours).

#### Teacher Qualifications

**Washington is the ONLY state requiring parent qualifications.** Must meet ONE of:
1. Earned at least **45 quarter college credits** (or equivalent)
2. Complete a state-approved **home instruction course**
3. Be **supervised** by a certified educator (at least 1 hour per week)
4. Be deemed "sufficiently qualified" by the local superintendent

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

**Yes (WIAA).** Homeschooled students can participate through the Washington Interscholastic Activities Association as "regular members" if they meet home-based instruction requirements and annually file a **WIAA Rule 18.6.3 form** with the principal's office.

#### Ancillary Services

Homeschoolers are eligible for free **ancillary services** including: counseling, psychological services, testing, remedial instruction, speech and hearing therapy, health-care services, and tutorial services.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. Washington does not issue a state diploma to homeschoolers, but parents may issue their own if high school credits meet state graduation requirements.

#### Key Organizations

- **Washington Homeschool Organization (WHO)** - washhomeschool.org - Statewide resource for laws, sports eligibility, getting started.
- **Christian Family Home Educators**
- **Christian Heritage Home Educators of Washington**

#### ESA/School Choice

**None.** Washington has no private school choice programs. No ESA, voucher, tax credit, or scholarship programs available for homeschoolers. Washington is one of only four western states (along with Idaho, Oregon, and California) without any ESA or education choice program.

### OKLAHOMA

#### Quick Facts: Homeschooling in Oklahoma

| Requirement | Oklahoma |
|-------------|----------|
| Compulsory Ages | 5-18 |
| Notification Required | No (recommended only) |
| Subjects Required | Yes - reading, writing, math, science, citizenship, more |
| Assessment Required | No |
| Teacher Qualifications | None |
| Regulation Level | None/Very Low |

#### Notification/Registration

- **No notification required** by law
- Homeschools are not regulated by the state
- No registration, approval, or inspection
- **Recommended** (but not required) to notify the local school principal
- **Only state where the right to homeschool is constitutionally protected** (Oklahoma Constitution, Art. XIII, Sec. 4)

#### Required Subjects

Must provide instruction in: reading, writing, math, science, citizenship, U.S. Constitution, health, safety, physical education, and conservation.

#### Assessment/Testing

No mandatory testing, evaluation, or assessment of any kind.

#### Instructional Requirements

Equivalent of **180 days** per calendar year, **6 clock hours (360 minutes) per day**, as appropriate for child's age.

#### Teacher Qualifications

None. No degree, certification, or educational requirements.

#### Sports/Extracurricular Access

**No.** The Oklahoma Secondary School Activities Association (OSSAA) prohibits homeschool students from participating in member school sports. "A student choosing to home school is not eligible for participation at a member school." Alternative: independent homeschool leagues such as the Oklahoma Homeschool Volleyball League (OHVL) and other city-based athletic programs.

#### Homeschool Diploma

Parent-issued. Colleges and employers generally accept parent-issued diplomas with detailed transcripts. No state record-keeping mandate.

#### Key Organizations

- **Homeschool Oklahoma (HSOK)** - homeschooloklahoma.org - Statewide Christian organization. Encouragement, support, planning resources.
- **OCHEC** - Statewide Christian support group focusing on legislative updates and getting-started resources.

#### ESA/School Choice

**Parental Choice Tax Credit:**
- **$1,000 per student** (homeschool) - claimed on tax return with receipts (Form 591-D)
- **$5 million annual cap** for homeschool credits
- Eligible expenses: curriculum, textbooks, online education, tutoring, testing fees, supplementary materials
- No application process - claimed directly on tax return
- Income AGI from two years prior is used for qualification
