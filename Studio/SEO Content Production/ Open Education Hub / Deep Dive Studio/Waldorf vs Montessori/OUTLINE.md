### H2: Key Differences

~400 words. The meat of the comparison.

**Subheads:**

#### Philosophy and Origins
- Montessori: scientific/medical background, observed children
- Waldorf: philosophical/spiritual background, anthroposophy
- Implication: different assumptions about childhood

#### Materials vs Rhythm
- Montessori is materials-driven (Pink Tower, Golden Beads)
- Waldorf is rhythm-driven (daily circles, seasonal festivals)
- Neither the materials nor the rhythm IS the pedagogy

#### Technology
- Waldorf explicitly limits screens
- Montessori treats tech as tool
- The real question: what serves your child?

#### Reading and Academics
- Both delay formal academics compared to traditional school
- Waldorf more explicit about delaying reading (age 7-8)
- Montessori: reading emerges from phonemic awareness
- Evidence: Finland, Waldorf schools show later readers catch up

### H2: FAQ

Targets People Also Ask. ~200 words.

**Q: Which is better, Waldorf or Montessori?**
Neither is objectively better. Waldorf emphasizes arts and rhythm; Montessori emphasizes independence and materials. The right choice depends on your child and family.

**Q: Is Waldorf religious?**
Waldorf has spiritual roots in anthroposophy, but schools vary. Some families find the philosophy enriching; others find it uncomfortable. Ask specific schools about their approach.

**Q: Is Montessori or Waldorf better for ADHD?**
Both can work. Montessori's self-paced structure and hands-on materials help some kids with ADHD. Waldorf's rhythm and movement may help others. The specific environment matters more than the label.

**Q: Can you combine Waldorf and Montessori?**
Yes. Many homeschool families take elements from both - Montessori practical life activities alongside Waldorf storytelling and nature connection.
