# Content Playbooks

## State Guide Template

**Length:** 2,000-2,500 words

**Structure:**
1. Quick Facts Table - ages, notification, assessment
2. How to Start - traditional vs OpenEd path
3. State Laws Breakdown
4. Funding Options - ESAs, reimbursement, tax credits
5. Curriculum Recommendations - link to grade guides
6. Local Resources - co-ops, microschools
7. FAQ Section (5-7 questions, schema markup)
8. CTA - enrollment or info session

**SEO:** Title "How to Homeschool in [State]: Complete Guide [Year]"

## Grade Level Guide Template

**Length:** 1,500-2,000 words

**Structure:**
1. Grade Overview - developmental context
2. OpenEd Recommended Stack - Math, Reading, LA, Science, History
3. Runner-Up Options
4. Free Resources
5. Sample Schedule
6. FAQ Section

**SEO:** Title "[Grade] Homeschool Curriculum: What Actually Works [Year]"

## Thinker Profile Template

**Length:** 1,500-2,000 words

**Structure:**
1. Opening Hook - compelling quote or story
2. Who They Were/Are - brief bio
3. Core Ideas - 3-5 key concepts
4. Why It Matters Today
5. Criticism & Counterpoints
6. How to Apply This
7. Further Reading

## Comparison Page Template

**Length:** 2,000-2,500 words

**Structure:**
1. TL;DR - quick recommendation by family type
2. Comparison Table - features, philosophy, cost, ages
3. [Option A] Explained
4. [Option B] Explained
5. Key Differences
6. Who Should Choose What
7. Can You Combine Them?
8. FAQ Section

## Tool Review Template

**Length:** 1,500-2,000 words

**Structure:**
1. Quick Verdict - rating, best for, price
2. What It Is
3. How It Works
4. What We Like
5. What Could Be Better
6. Pricing
7. Alternatives
8. Who It's Best For
9. OpenEd Teacher Take

# Distribution Strategy

## State Guides → Social

**Primary:** Facebook (local groups), LinkedIn (credibility)

- Facebook: Post to state-specific homeschool groups
- LinkedIn: "We just published the most comprehensive guide to homeschooling in Nevada..."
- X: Thread format with 5-7 key facts
- Email: Segment by state

## Grade Guides → Social

**Primary:** Facebook (parent communities), Pinterest (curriculum planning)

- Facebook: Grade-specific groups, "What curriculum are you using?" posts
- Pinterest: Curriculum stack visual pins
- Instagram: "5 must-haves for [grade]" carousel
- Email: Back-to-school (July-Aug), mid-year (Jan)

## Thinkers → Social

**Primary:** LinkedIn (thought leadership), X (intellectual discourse)

- LinkedIn: Contrarian insight + relevance, link in comments
- X: Thread of key ideas as provocative statements
- Newsletter: Feature in "Thought" section
- Podcast: Reference in episodes

## Comparison Pages → Social

**Primary:** All platforms (high search = high share)

- Facebook: Post to method-specific groups, poll format
- LinkedIn: "The difference isn't what you think"
- X: Hot take format
- Pinterest: Comparison infographic
- Instagram: Swipe-through decision tree

## Tool Reviews → Social

**Primary:** Facebook (recommendations), YouTube (demos)

- Facebook: Curriculum review groups
- YouTube: Walkthrough videos for high-volume tools
- Instagram: Reel with "3 things I wish I knew"
- Email: "Tool of the Week" segment
